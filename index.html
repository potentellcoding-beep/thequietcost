The Quiet Cost

A Stepparent's Journey Through Parental Alienation

A gripping, unfiltered account of Parental Alienation, told through the eyes of the person who is always present, always invested, and yet always unseen: the stepparent.

Get the Paperback Released March 4, 2026

The Quiet Cost follows one woman’s fourteen-year journey through the hidden world of parental alienation, where love becomes a battlefield, truth is rewritten, and children are quietly pulled away through manipulation, guilt, and emotional control.

What begins with “small inconveniences” grows into a relentless storm. Scheduled sabotage, rewritten memories, false accusations, psychological games, and the slow, devastating erasure of a parent-child bond. As a new marriage forms and a blended family begins to take shape, so does a dangerous dynamic: a high-conflict ex determined to divide loyalties and dominate the narrative at any cost.

Through court battles, years of documentation, escalating jealousy, and the heartbreaking loss of a once-loving relationship with a stepson, the author uncovers the truth she never knew had a name: Parental Alienation.

This book exposes the patterns alienating parents use:

Subtle Control Constant Undermining Gatekeeping Emotional Tethering Triangulation False Narratives Rewriting a Child’s Reality

A mirror held up to a painful but common experience
